# Teacher-Facing Review Deck Outline
## AI, the Brain, and Serviam

Purpose: This is a leader-facing companion deck. It explains the rationale, research base, pedagogy, standards alignment, and review logic behind the student-facing Gamma deck. It should match the student deck without becoming the student lesson.

## 1. Why this lesson
Leigh Ann requested a 20-minute student-facing lesson that gives a flavor of AI, how it works, and judicious use. This plan keeps the lesson teachable while the appendix shows depth.

## 2. The core thesis
AI is not magic. It is math trained on patterns. Human wisdom decides whether, when, and how to use it.

## 3. Ursuline fit
The lesson connects AI literacy to Serviam, intellectual curiosity, respect, compassion, integrity, whole-person education, and young women as builders, critics, and leaders.

## 4. What students experience
Students predict, discuss, define, trace a prompt, play a probability game, learn attention, audit an AI answer, apply SERVIAM, and complete an exit ticket.

## 5. Technical accuracy
The lesson accurately introduces tokens, numerical representation, probability, attention, model variation, and human review while avoiding overstatement.

## 6. Brain science and learning
The lesson uses prediction, retrieval, metacognition, chunking, worked examples, student talk, and a reflective close.

## 7. Critical thinking in action
Students do not merely hear that AI can be wrong. They audit a flawed AI answer and revise the prompt.

## 8. Judicious use
The SERVIAM protocol teaches students to start with their own thinking, examine purpose, require reasoning, verify claims, identify risk, attribute assistance, and make AI serve learning.

## 9. Differentiation
Grades 7 and 8 focus on patterns, prediction, verification, and safe use. Grades 9 and 10 add tokens, probability, attention, and bias. AP students can extend into vectors, model training, and system design.

## 10. Standards alignment
Aligned to CSTA AI priorities, AI4K12 Five Big Ideas, AP CSP computational thinking and impacts, AP CSA conceptual extensions, digital citizenship, math, science, and Catholic-school mission.

## 11. Similar-school scan
Andover, Exeter, Lawrenceville, Castilleja, Hockaday, Marlborough, Ursuline Dallas, Notre Dame ACE, and Catholic Education Services point toward guided use, transparency, AI literacy, human-centered design, and mission-based ethics.

## 12. Optional live AI use
Live AI can be used for prompt surgery, but prepared examples are safer for a 20-minute sample. No personal information should be entered.

## 13. Stress test
The plan avoids being too broad, too technical, too generic, too ethics-only, too tool-focused, or too research-heavy.

## 14. Closing recommendation
Submit the student Gamma deck, lesson plan, teacher-facing rationale deck, student SERVIAM handout, and Brett private script packet as a coherent package.
