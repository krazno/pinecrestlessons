# Teacher Review Outline
## AI, the Brain, and Serviam

Purpose: Leader-facing companion for Part 1. It explains the rationale, research base, pedagogy, standards alignment, and delivery logic behind the student lesson.

## 1. Why this lesson
A 20-minute student-facing lesson on AI literacy, how language models work at a high level, and judicious use. The plan stays teachable while supporting deeper extension in Part 2.

## 2. The core thesis
AI is not magic. It is math trained on patterns. Human wisdom decides whether, when, and how to use it.

## 3. Ursuline fit
The lesson connects AI literacy to Serviam, intellectual curiosity, respect, compassion, integrity, whole-person education, and young women as builders, critics, and leaders.

## 4. What students experience
Students predict, discuss, define, trace a prompt, play a probability game, learn attention, audit an AI answer, apply SERVIAM, and complete an exit ticket.

## 5. Technical accuracy
The lesson introduces tokens, numerical representation, probability, attention, model variation, and human review without overstatement.

## 6. Brain science and learning
The lesson uses prediction, retrieval, metacognition, chunking, worked examples, student talk, and a reflective close.

## 7. Critical thinking in action
Students audit a flawed AI answer and revise the prompt instead of only hearing that AI can be wrong.

## 8. Judicious use
The SERVIAM protocol teaches students to start with their own thinking, examine purpose, require reasoning, verify claims, identify risk, attribute assistance, and make AI serve learning.

## 9. Differentiation
Grades 7 and 8 focus on patterns, prediction, verification, and safe use. Grades 9 and 10 add tokens, probability, attention, and bias. Part 2 extends vectors, weights, and training for upper grades.

## 10. Standards alignment
Aligned to CSTA AI priorities, AI4K12 Five Big Ideas, AP CSP computational thinking and impacts, AP CSA conceptual extensions, digital citizenship, math, science, and mission-based ethics.

## 11. Delivery notes
Live AI can be used for prompt surgery, but prepared examples are safer for a 20-minute sample. No personal information should be entered during class demos.

## 12. Package contents
Student slide deck, student SERVIAM handout, teacher lesson plan, and this review outline form one coherent Part 1 package.
