# Part 2 Alignment Checklist

## Ready
- 12-slide deck structure.
- 15 to 20 minute timing.
- Grade 10/11 target with AP extension notes.
- Clear extension of Part 1, not a replacement.
- Core thesis matches Part 1: AI is powerful because it can generate. Human judgment determines whether that output is accurate, ethical, and useful.
- Technical arc: function, data, tokens, vectors, similarity, weights, loss, probability, attention, generation loop, evaluation.
- Mission closure through Serviam: Does this serve?
- Student handout included.
- Teacher lesson plan included.
- Research source map included.

## Deck polish notes
- Audience is 10th/11th grade, not AP-only.
- Reduce text density on slides 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 10, and 11.
- Remove emojis from Slide 4.
- Replace "vectors represent meaning mathematically" with "vectors represent learned relationships mathematically."
- Replace "encode semantic meaning" with "help represent relationships learned from data."
- Fix the attention formula if used, or move it to speaker notes.
- Replace awkward example sentences with clear classroom language.
- Replace "a trustworthy AI model must pass all seven" with "these are evaluation dimensions for responsible AI use."
- Avoid em dashes in final submission files.

## Do not include in student-facing materials
- Graduate-level derivations.
- Heavy matrix notation beyond optional notes.
- Claims that AI thinks, knows, understands, remembers, feels, or has judgment.
- Cyberpunk, neon, hacker, scary AI, or cartoon robot imagery.
- Mixed-gender or male imagery.
- Raw URLs on student-facing slides.

## Framing
Part 1 teaches students how to use AI wisely. Part 2 shows what is happening mathematically and computationally under the hood.
